Fast Fashion: Environmental Impact in Canada
Understanding the Hidden Cost of Cheap Clothing

TOPIC STATEMENT
Fast fashion — the rapid production of inexpensive, trend-driven clothing — drives a linear "take-make-dispose" model with severe environmental consequences. Canadians purchase an estimated 70 garments per person annually, yet the average item is worn only 7–10 times. The fashion industry accounts for 8–10% of global carbon emissions — more than international flights and shipping combined.

KEY STATISTICS
- Canadians send approximately 12 kilograms of textile waste per person per year to landfill.
- It takes roughly 2,700 litres of water to produce a single cotton T-shirt.
- The global fashion industry emits approximately 1.2 gigatonnes of CO₂ per year.
- Less than 1% of clothing is recycled into new garments worldwide.

ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACTS IN CANADA

Carbon Emissions
Fashion produces ~1.2 Gt CO₂/yr globally. Synthetic fibres from fossil fuels generate emissions during production and incineration.

Water Pollution
Fashion consumes ~93 billion m³ of water/yr. Textile dyeing is the 2nd-largest water polluter, introducing heavy metals into freshwater.

Landfill & Waste
Canadians send ~12 kg of textile waste per person to landfills annually. Synthetics take 20–200 years to decompose.

Biodiversity Loss
Cotton uses 6% of global pesticides and 16% of insecticides. Fibre crop expansion drives deforestation globally.

SOLUTIONS: HOW YOU CAN HELP

Buy Less, Choose Well
Invest in durable garments — reducing purchases by 30% could cut emissions equal to removing 1M cars.

Thrift & Swap
Shop second-hand or organize clothing swaps to extend garment lifespans.

Recycle & Donate
Use textile recycling programs — many Canadian cities offer curbside collection.

Wash Mindfully
Cold water and microfibre bags reduce microplastic release by up to 80%.

Advocate for Policy
Support extended producer responsibility (EPR) under Canada's Zero Plastic Waste strategy.

Sustainable Fabrics
Choose organic cotton, TENCEL™, or recycled fibres with GOTS/OEKO-TEX certification.
